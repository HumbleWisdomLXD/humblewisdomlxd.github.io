Crisis Leadership Scenario

Score: 0/3

The Situation

You're the Clinical Director overseeing three mental health teams. It's Monday morning, and you've just discovered that your highest-performing team is falling apart. Over the weekend, two key psychologists submitted resignation letters, citing "toxic team dynamics" and "lack of support."

This team handles your most complex cases, including several high-risk clients. The team lead, Dr. Sarah Chen, has been with the organization for 5 years and was recently promoted. She's scheduled to meet with you in 30 minutes, visibly upset and demanding immediate answers.

Your challenge: You need to make three critical decisions that will determine whether this situation becomes a learning opportunity that strengthens your organization, or a crisis that damages your reputation and client care.

Remember: Your decisions should reflect our core values

Empowerment • Transparency • Excellence

The Moment:

Dr. Chen arrives at your office early, clearly distressed. She immediately starts: "I don't understand what went wrong. I've been doing everything by the book, but now everyone's saying I'm a terrible leader. I feel like I'm failing everyone - the team, the clients, the organization."

How do you respond in this moment?

A
"Let's focus on fixing this quickly. I need you to call each team member and convince them to stay. We can't afford to lose more people."
❌Leadership Insight: This reactive approach pressures Dr. Chen without addressing root causes and misses the opportunity to build her leadership capacity.

Value Analysis: Lacks Empowerment - you're directing rather than developing her problem-solving skills.
B
"I can see you're really struggling with this. Before we dive into solutions, help me understand what you think led to this situation. What's your perspective on what happened?"
✅Exceptional Leadership Choice! This response demonstrates authentic Empowerment by creating psychological safety and seeking understanding first.

Value in Action: You're building Dr. Chen's analytical capacity and treating her as a capable leader who can contribute to solutions.
C
"This is a serious situation. I'm going to need to interview each team member individually to get the full story before we proceed."
❌Leadership Insight: While investigation is important, bypassing Dr. Chen undermines her role and misses a critical development opportunity.

Value Analysis: Lacks Empowerment - you're taking control rather than building her investigative and leadership skills.
D
"Leadership is tough, and sometimes these things happen. Let's just reassign the clients to other teams and start fresh with new staff."
❌Leadership Insight: This dismissive approach ignores valuable learning opportunities and fails to address systemic issues.

Value Analysis: Misses both Empowerment and Excellence - not developing people or improving organizational systems.
