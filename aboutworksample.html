Adult Learning Theory Foundation

This training was intentionally designed using evidence-based adult learning principles to maximize engagement, retention, and practical application. Our approach integrates multiple established theories that research shows are most effective for adult learners.

ðŸ§  Learning Theory Integration

Rather than using a single approach, we've combined the most effective elements from three major adult learning theories to create a comprehensive, research-backed learning experience.

1. Andragogy (Malcolm Knowles) - Self-Directed Learning

Theory Application: Six Principles of Adult Learning

Need to Know: Training opens by explaining why values matter for daily work collaboration

Experience Foundation: Scenarios use real workplace situations from actual exit interviews

Self-Concept: Learners choose their role and have autonomy in discovery process

Readiness: Addresses immediate workplace challenges employees actually face

Problem-Centered: Focuses on solving real issues rather than abstract concepts

Internal Motivation: Connects to personal job satisfaction and career success

2. Experiential Learning Theory (David Kolb) - Learning Through Experience

Theory Application: Four-Stage Learning Cycle

Concrete Experience: Interactive scenarios with real workplace decisions

Reflective Observation: Coaching feedback helps learners analyze their choices

Abstract Conceptualization: Values revelation shows underlying principles

Active Experimentation: Inter-role scenarios let learners apply concepts in new situations

3. Transformative Learning Theory (Jack Mezirow) - Perspective Change

Theory Application: Critical Reflection and Transformation

Disorienting Dilemma: Exit interview data reveals gap between values and practice

Critical Reflection: Learners examine their own assumptions about workplace behavior

Perspective Transformation: Inductive discovery creates new understanding of values

Integration: New perspective guides future workplace decisions

Research-Based Design Choices:

Inductive vs. Deductive Learning: Research shows adults prefer discovering principles themselves rather than being told what to think. Our pattern-recognition approach leverages this preference.
Problem-Based Learning: Adults learn best when content addresses real needs for real-world situations, which is why we used actual workplace scenarios from exit data.
Experience-Centered Design: Kolb's research demonstrates that learning occurs through the transformation of experience, not passive information intake, supporting our interactive scenario approach.
Critical Reflection: Mezirow found that adults can transform their understanding through critical reflection on assumptions, which occurs in our values discovery process.

Instructional Design Methodology

1. Inductive Learning Methodology

Why: Exit data showed employees felt "talked at" rather than engaged. Inductive discovery creates personal investment in the values.

How: Employees group workplace examples and derive values themselves, then see official definitions.

2. Role-Specific Scenarios

Why: Different roles face different challenges. Generic training doesn't address specific workplace realities.

How: Three distinct scenario paths based on actual role-specific issues from exit data.

3. Real Workplace Situations

Why: Exit interviews revealed specific pain points. Training needed to address actual problems, not hypothetical ones.

How: Scenarios directly incorporate themes from exit data: leadership disconnect, toxic culture, workload pressure.

4. Values-Pairs Structure

Why: Single values can be abstract. Paired values provide context and practical application guidance.

How: Six value pairs that work together: Empowerment/Collaboration, Integrity/Transparency, Innovation/Growth, Humility/Respect, Partnership/Security, Excellence/Commitment.

5. Inter-Role Collaboration Focus

Why: Exit data showed breakdown in cross-department relationships and communication.

How: Second scenario set focuses specifically on how roles work together, addressing actual collaboration failures.

Evidence-Based Features

Addressing Specific Exit Interview Concerns:

Expected Outcomes

This training is designed to address the root causes identified in our exit data:

  • Reduced Leadership Disconnect: Values-based decision making creates consistency between stated and practiced values
  • Improved Cross-Role Collaboration: Shared values language and problem-solving approaches
  • Stronger Culture Foundation: Values become actionable guides rather than wall decorations
  • Enhanced Client Care: Internal culture strength directly impacts external service quality

Measurement & Follow-Up

Success will be measured through:

  • Follow-up pulse surveys using similar questions to exit interviews
  • Retention rates by role and department
  • Cross-department collaboration indicators
  • Client satisfaction scores correlation with staff engagement
